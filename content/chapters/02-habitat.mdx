A truncated icosahedron — **twenty hexagons and twelve pentagons** — with
a fixed outer diameter of **11.151 m** and a single mechanical language
repeated across every facet.

The habitat is not shaped this way for aesthetics. It is the only
polyhedron that reaches this diameter from **32 panels** with **90
edges** all at the same canonical length — **2.25 m** — while packing
structure, glazing, and life support behind one repeatable joint.

**Transparency.** Modular glazing for situational awareness and
well-being; not a windowless tube. Every hex and non-docking pent is a
structurally regular face driven off the shell — when the shell moves,
its modules move with it.

**Geometry, not decoration.** Repeatable panel logic across 32 faces
means one validated joint unlocks the whole habitat. Precedents don't —
every ISS module has its own seal story.

**Systems core, not a bolt-on.** An opaque central volume for ECLSS and
power, paired with hybrid bio-physicochemical life support. The
[axial core](#eclss-water) runs along the principal axis and keeps the
habitat a single pressure boundary, not a module stack.

**Docking is topology-level.** Exactly one pentagon is the docking face.
Not a port glued next to a closed pent — the docking ring, collar,
struts, and tunnel shell replace that pent's normal stackup. Alignment
is driven by the face normal of the chosen pent; deviation from that
normal is a validation failure.
