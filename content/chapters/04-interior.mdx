Perimeter volume against the transparent shell — observation, exercise,
social space — with real connection to Earth and sky. An opaque service
core at the centre houses racks, water processing, power conversion,
and stowage: the machine that keeps the observatory breathable.

About **~520–560 m³** of habitable volume, organized into **eight zones**
that can be reconfigured without touching the pressure boundary.

### The eight zones

| Zone | Where | Why at the perimeter / core |
|---|---|---|
| Sleep / private quarters | Perimeter | Horizon view, personal orientation |
| Exercise | Perimeter | Line-of-sight Earth during cardio |
| Crew social / wardroom | Perimeter | Shared window time |
| Observation deck | Equatorial perimeter | Longest uninterrupted window arc |
| Greenhouse | 1 pent per zone | Light + CO₂ access to shell |
| ECLSS + water | Core | Plumbing centrality, mass concentration |
| Power + thermal | Core | Loads to the axial beam |
| Stowage / service | Core | Unoccupied volume, packing density |

**Decoupled reconfiguration.** Because the interior zones attach to the
core and to face drivers — not to the pressure shell itself — a mission
crew can re-lay the habitat without breaching the hull. A sleep quarter
today can be a lab next rotation; neither move requires an EVA.

**Perimeter for humans, core for machines.** The transparent shell is
where crew spend awake time. The opaque core absorbs noise, vibration,
and the visual chaos of racks and cabling. Psychological volume lives
outside. Mechanical volume lives inside.

**Mass in, volume out.** At 11.151 m outer diameter the internal volume
approaches 726 m³ theoretical — the ~520–560 m³ figure is the
pressurised, usable envelope after core and structural allowances.

### Not a windowless tube

Every hex and non-docking pent is a viewport unless re-assigned to a
greenhouse, shield, or utility role. The [panel stackup](#panel-v3) lets
any face be tuned between transparent and opaque via the
electrochromic layer without swapping hardware — so a module reconfig
is software + shade state, not a hull mod.

Next you'll see a single hex come apart into its seven layers — proof
that "transparent" is an engineering stack, not wishful thinking.
