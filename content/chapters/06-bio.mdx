Physico-chemical processors handle peaks and reliability. Plants and
algae provide partial food, CO₂ drawdown, and the psychological
presence of something alive.

Pentagon facets host concentrated greenhouse functions without breaking
the hex repetition elsewhere — **one pent per zone, three trays each,
full-spectrum LED banks**. Scroll past this phase and you'll see one of
them hinge outward 90° so the grow volume reads as architecture, not
cargo.

### The gas budget

| Flow | Per crew · day | Role |
|---|---|---|
| O₂ generated | **~0.84 kg** | Partial metabolic coverage |
| CO₂ produced (captured) | **~1.0 kg** | Must be removed from the air |
| Plant biomass | Partial | Psychological + fresh food |
| Water transpired | Returns to closed loop | See §Thermal & Water |

A pure-bio ECLSS at closure would need far more plant volume than a
habitat this size can give. HCSA is therefore **hybrid**: bio takes the
baseline load, physico-chemical covers peaks and failures.

### What the pentagon lets us do

Pentagons are the only non-hex face on the shell. That makes them the
natural home for subsystems that need **concentrated infrastructure**
(lighting, fluid feeds, drainage) without disrupting the hex
repetition elsewhere. Four pent families coexist in the design:

- **Plant-lit pentagon** — greenhouse (this phase)
- **Shield-host / service pentagon** — stowage for deployable shields
- **Standard service pentagon** — storage, utilities, airflow
- **Shield-deployed pentagon** — pump + stowed inflatable radiation
  shield canister; when inflated, the shield occupies the exterior side
  of that pentagon

One pent is the docking face — not a greenhouse candidate.

### Why you see **green** only here

The cyan wireframe reads as "structural truth" across the whole tour.
The biology-green emissive is reserved for the Phase 6 grow lights — so
when you see it, you know the pentagon is alive, not just lit.
