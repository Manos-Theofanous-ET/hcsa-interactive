Staged evidence from parametric CAD to full-scale coupons — so the
architecture is reviewable, not just renderable. Nothing here is a
structural claim until a physical coupon carries its share of load.

### P1 → P7

| Stage | Scope | Status |
|---|---|---|

| **P2** | Digital validation — FEA, CFD, thermal | Complete |

### What P5 has to prove

The current coupon is the first article at actual edge length and
actual frame depth.

- **Preload.** Lockbolt torque curves across NAS9306C-06 at Ø 4.83 mm,
  14 fasteners over 550 mm at ~39 mm pitch.
- **Leak rate.** Dual fluorosilicone primary seal + RTV tertiary,
  pressurized to one atmosphere, held at vacuum delta. Target reaches
  ISS threshold.
- **Assembly repeatability.** Field-swappable. If a panel can't be
  swapped in gloves, the design fails here, not in orbit.

### What stays honest

- **FEA + physical correlation** still required before structural
  claims are treated as validated. Until a coupon matches the model,
  the model is a hypothesis.
- **ECLSS closure** remains provisional. The plant + physico-chemical
  hybrid has been modelled at steady state; failure-mode closure is
  unproven.
- **MMOD and radiation** are conservative design allowances, not
  tested numbers. Shields are in the pent family for this reason.

### What that means for a reviewer

Anything you see in the earlier phases that looks finished — the
geometry, the seal stack, the greenhouse layout — is finished at the
**design** level. The **engineering** level is where P5 is currently
operating. P6 and P7 haven't happened.
